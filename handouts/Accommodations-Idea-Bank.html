Personalized one-page summary (sample case)

Generated from a case, not written from scratch. Meant to print or drop into notes, and does not require coming back to the website.

Who this is for

Client: 9-year-old, FASD-informed brain-based profile. Caregiver: adoptive mother, involved in treatment.

Brain-based profile at a glance

Client

Executive function: Moderate Processing speed: Moderate Social & relational: Mild

Caregiver

Affect regulation: Mild Executive function: Non-existent

Treatment modality and what it asks of the brain

PCIT. Asks the client's executive function and processing speed (following commands, complying within a timed window) and the caregiver's affect regulation and consistency (staying calm and delivering skills the same way every time).

Treatment goal: Caregiver will use PRIDE skills during CDI sessions, breaking any request into one simple step at a time so the child can follow it.

The different parts of treatment

  • CDI (Child-Directed Interaction): caregiver follows the child's lead and narrates play using PRIDE skills.
  • PDI (Parent-Directed Interaction): caregiver gives commands and the child is expected to comply within a set window.
  • Time-out sequence: the consequence used when a command is not followed.

Guiding questions for accommodations (work through these, don't just read an answer)

  1. What is this part of treatment actually asking the brain to do?
  2. Does the client or caregiver have that capacity right now, based on what we know about them?
  3. If not, what could change: the pace, the format, the environment, who's responsible, or the goal itself?
  4. What's one idea you haven't tried yet?
  5. What will look different in the very next session because of this?
